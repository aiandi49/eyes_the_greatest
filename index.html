She printed her own press pass at 18 and walked into New York like she'd been hired. No one ever told her otherwise.

Before she was a brand, she was a kid setting scenes with her toys. Before she was a survivor of nine surgeries, she was a girl with a police scanner and a best friend named Kylie, chasing sirens with a camcorder because the story wouldn't come to her. This is E.Myers — visionary, creator, and the greatest, in her own right — answering the only five questions that have ever mattered: who, what, when, where, and why.

W
Question One
Who is Erica?

Who is the daughter of Gloria Myers? Who is the lady who made beautiful music at 352 Mattianic Avenue? Who is E.Myers?

I would describe Erica as a visionary. I always had an imagination, and I always acted on it — even going back to the early '80s, when I was a child. The way I played with my toys, it was always setting up a scene of some sort. I've always had a very inquisitive mind — I needed to know what the deal was. I'm laid back, not shy, but people perceive it as shy because I'm such an observer. When I go quiet, I'm watching.

I'm the type of person who likes to try new things, likes to learn new things, and with the way technology is now, I can learn on my own. I don't need instruction from a human — I can go online and figure it out myself. Who Erica is, at the core, is someone who likes to create. I am a creator. If I didn't have any technology, if I didn't have a camera, if I didn't have any way to make music, I would still create something — even if I had to write it in the sand.

W
Question Two
What is “Eyes the Greatest”?

What's the one factor about Erica that people of all ages should take notice of — the thing that could help them better themselves?

What I the Greatest represents, to me, is realness. I'm a real person — I don't fake the funk, I don't sugarcoat, and I always turn a negative into a positive. Growing up I used to get picked on for how big my eyes were. It never bothered me. I always liked Muhammad Ali — he was always saying I'm the greatest — and when I was seventeen, my friends started calling me “Eyes.” To this day there are people in this world who call me Eyes like that's my real name. I put the two together: Eyes the Greatest.

The brand holds everything I love to do as a visionary — music, artistry, videography, photography, production. When I first started carrying a camera, and later, around eighteen, my best friend Kylie and I would grab a police scanner and show up at scenes — accidents, whatever it was — and run our own news segment. She was my camera person, I had my little mic. When I got to New York I printed my own press pass at a Kinko's and walked around the city as “I The Greatest Productions,” showing up like I was the real deal. Because I was.

"I was the greatest before it was a brand — it started back when I was a teenager with a scanner and a borrowed camera."

W
Question Three
When did she become who she is?

When was Erica born? When did she fail, and when did she rise? When does she decide what's right for herself and the people around her?

Erica's inception into the world was because God made her great before she ever became human. I came here to be of service — to be of help to the masses. That's why I was born to Gloria Myers. From 1980 all the way to 2026, that's forty-six and a half years, and there have been ups and downs like everyone's life. I could say the good outweighed the bad, or the bad outweighed the good — but I don't want to say that, because I'm an optimist.

2001
First surgery she remembers — tonsils out, age twenty-one.
2009
A brutal, painful surgery that taught her she could take anything.
2014
Lumbar fusion. "That changed everything." Had to learn to walk again — literally, in a class, from scratch.
2014–2026
Nine surgeries and counting, including a hysterectomy, while showing up for a mother going through breast cancer.

Those aren't failures to me. They're mishaps — obstacles that got in the way of the life I thought I'd be living. But I'm thankful, because I don't have to be at an office with a bunch of assholes, or any job, even a so-called dream job. There's no such thing as a dream job. There are things you're capable of, things you're good at — and if you can do what you love and get paid well for it, I guess that's what people mean.

"I don't have to be three-quarters full as long as it's not empty. It's still time to refill the cup."

W
Question Four
Where is she going?

Where is Erica now? Where has she been? Where does she find herself when she takes her last breath?

I used to have a definitive answer for that. Right now, I don't — just slight speculations. So many things have changed that I'm just thankful. I'm not too proud to beg, and I've been saying that ever since I used to go to 7 Times Square every day for work. That's something I've never fully had in my life: peace. So where I see myself is somewhere I don't have to worry — am I going to go broke, is there going to be enough. I see myself wherever peace is.

Where my last breath will be, I don't know. I hope it's not in Connecticut, but who the fuck knows. If it was, I'm okay with that too — I feel like I've lived a full life, and a couple of lifetimes on top of it. Sometimes in my dreams I feel like I've gone fifty, a hundred years overnight, and come back — almost like Back to the Future. So, where I see myself is somewhere sitting in peace. As long as I get to peace, that's where I need to be.

W
Question Five
Why does Erica exist?

Why was Erica born? Why is she standing up for herself and others? Why is she alive today, and why can't anyone manipulate her?

Why I exist, why I'm melanated the way I am, is because I'm a true representation of what a soul is — of who and how God has made someone. I'm pretty much a full mirror of the world. People can see what they don't want to see about themselves in me, or not. That's the reason I get a lot of hate — you can't let things bounce off your shoulders and dissipate into the air the way I can, and it makes you feel better to hate on a person like me for it.

I believe I was brought to this earth to show people that you can be a real person and still be of service. I'm a representation of truth — of how I want to be, with free reign to do so. I don't need validation, because me being able to just be is validation enough. I'm here, talking about it now. That's validation enough.

So that's why I believe I exist. I exist because I have helped so much in my life — Vincent and his kids, Gloria, Lank, whether things went well or not. I've always been of service to people. When I die, people can lie about a lot of things, but it would be a damn lie to say I wasn't always there for somebody. I exist because I have something great to give this world, and I think I've already given most of it. The next chapter is for me to be at peace — because I've carried the weight of the world my whole life. And that's why.

In Conclusion
The Vision

If this became an app, an online journal, a place for people who need help but don't know it yet — what would it be?

It's a lesson learned within self. For the Bible thumpers out there, Proverbs talks about leaning on God and not on your own understanding — and I believe that's what this app could be. What it can do for people, because it starts with me, is let them answer their own W's: who, what, when, where, why. That's going to help them understand who they are, and take themselves out of what society looks like, and really dig deep into who they really are.

For every good thing I think about, I can think of three bad things. For every bad thing, I can think of five good things. My first car broke down when I didn't want it to — and in that same thought, I can remember the first time we rode the monorail at Epcot. There are so many layers to good and bad behaviors, good and bad memories. This application, starting with me, will show people: take yourself, take your feelings, take your transgressions out of this world, and dig deeper into yourself. That's what it's here to do — self-help, for yourself, by yourself.

People are afraid to confront themselves. Someone can sit in sorrow, sit in unhappiness, because they won't allow themselves to be great — they'd rather look in the mirror and say I'm unworthy. When I look in the mirror, I don't see that. I see good. I tell myself: Erica, you're doing your thing. You're not going to give up. You're great. You have more to give the world before you ever take your last breath. That's the conversation I have with myself in the mirror, every time.

"It's just a more in-depth self-help application for the masses, starting with Erica — with me. To help people start invoking, and promising, and invoking positivity, because the world is so hooked on negative actions and negative entities."

I'm always gonna be me.
— Erica “Eyes” Myers, a dope woman, still writing the next chapter
